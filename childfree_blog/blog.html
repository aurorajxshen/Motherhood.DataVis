Why a Generation is Saying No to Having Kids

In 1960, women in the U.S. had, on average, 3.6 children. In 2023? That number dropped to just 1.62—far below replacement levels.

I was doing an NLP homework and wanted to scrape and analyze comments from a Reddit discussion that caught my attention.

This analysis explores financial, climate, and cultural factors behind the birth rate decline, supported by real Reddit comments and data visualizations.
